Draft Writing

←
→

In many older weaving drafts, the treadling is not marked with dots, crosses or squares. Instead the movements are written out, or drawn, as continual lines almost as a form of asemic, cursive, handwriting. The points "selecting" specific treadles are integrated into flowing lines of movement.

This abstraction is an attempt to think about those motions, these streaks and paths that flow from hand to draft to weave.

Draw into the horizontal field next to the matrix by touch/drag or click/drag to start generating patterns from drawing/writing by. And simalarily, touch or click in the matrix to change the setup.
